Provenance of the figures

A site that weighs other people's evidence has to show its own. Every figure used anywhere on the site is listed below, with the study it comes from and a link to the paper — checked against the source, so nothing floats free of a citation and you can always read further.

A note on the arithmetic: a few figures (the roulette house edge, slot return-to-player) are not study results at all but published design parameters or definitions — specifications rather than estimates, which is why they need no study to back them.

Sources

Smoking

  • Doll & Hill, BMJ 1950;2:739 — hospital case-control, 649 lung cancer cases.
  • Doll et al., BMJ 2004;328:1519 (British Doctors Study) — ~10 years of life lost; cessation at 60/50/40/30 gaining ~3/6/9/10 years; 34,439 doctors, 50 years' follow-up.
  • Pirie et al., Lancet 2013;381:133 (Million Women Study) — stopping before 40 avoids >90% of excess mortality; before 30, >97%.
  • Jha et al., NEJM 2013;368:341 — reproduces the ~10-year deficit in a US cohort.
  • Banks et al., BMC Med 2015;13:38 — roughly two-thirds of lifelong smokers killed by smoking.
  • Denissenko et al., Science 1996;274:430 — benzo[a]pyrene adducts at TP53 codons 157, 248, 273.
  • Bradford Hill, Proc R Soc Med 1965;58:295 — the viewpoints paper on causal inference.
  • Lindson et al., Cochrane 2024;CD010216 — high-certainty evidence that nicotine e-cigarettes help established smokers quit.
  • Stead et al., Cochrane 2016;CD008286 — combined pharmacotherapy + behavioural support vs minimal support, RR 1.83.
  • Hartmann-Boyce et al., Cochrane 2019;CD009670 — additional behavioural support as an adjunct to medication, RR 1.15.
  • Hartmann-Boyce et al., Cochrane 2018;CD000146 — nicotine replacement therapy vs control, RR 1.55.
  • Livingstone-Banks et al., Cochrane 2023;CD006103 — nicotine-receptor partial agonists: varenicline RR 2.32, cytisine RR 1.30.
  • Cahill et al., Cochrane 2013;CD009329 — network meta-analysis of pharmacological options; varenicline and combination NRT most effective.

Alcohol

  • Zhao, Stockwell, Naimi et al., JAMA Netw Open 2023;6:e236185 — 107 cohort studies, 4.8 million participants; no significant mortality reduction below 25 g/day; increased mortality at ≥25 g/day in women and ≥45 g/day in men; corrected May 2023.
  • Millwood et al., Lancet 2019;393:1831 (China Kadoorie Biobank) — ~500,000 participants; ALDH2 / ADH1B instruments; monotonic linear stroke risk.
  • Collaborative Group on Hormonal Factors in Breast Cancer, Br J Cancer 2002 — breast cancer risk ~7% per 10 g/day of alcohol.
  • IARC Monograph 100E — alcoholic beverages classified Group 1 (carcinogenic to humans).
  • UK Chief Medical Officers' low-risk drinking guidelines, 2016 — no level completely safe; keep to ≤14 units/week.
  • Canada's Guidance on Alcohol and Health, CCSA 2023 — a continuum of risk rather than a threshold.

Gambling

  • Wardle et al., Lancet Public Health 2024 (Commission on Gambling) — 46.2% of adults and 17.9% of adolescents gambled in the past year; 10.3% of adolescents online; any-risk gambling in 5.5% of women and 11.9% of men; ~448.7 million adults affected; gambling disorder in 15.8% of online casino/slot players versus 8.9% of sports bettors; net losses near US$700 billion by 2028; legal in >80% of countries.
  • Muggleton et al., Nat Hum Behav 2021;5:319 — 6.5 million UK bank customers; dose-responsive financial distress and mortality.
  • Karlsson & Håkansson, J Behav Addict 2018 — ~15-fold elevated suicide rate in gambling disorder (Swedish registers).
  • Gambling design parameters (definitions / published specifications, not estimates) — single-zero roulette house edge 2.7% (= 1/37); double-zero 5.26% (= 2/38); UK online slots return-to-player typically 92–96% (a specified 4–8% retention); National Lottery returns roughly half of stakes as prizes.

Food

  • Dale et al., Circulation 2017;135:2373 — Mendelian randomisation: higher adiposity causally increases coronary heart disease, stroke and type 2 diabetes.
  • Lauby-Secretan et al. (IARC Working Group), NEJM 2016;375:794 — sufficient evidence that excess body fatness causes thirteen cancers.
  • Global BMI Mortality Collaboration, Lancet 2016;388:776 — ~3.9 million adults; all-cause mortality lowest at BMI 20–25, ~31% higher per 5 kg/m² above 25.
  • Simmonds et al., Obes Rev 2016;17:95 — obesity tracks from childhood; children with obesity ~5× as likely to have it as adults, ~80% of adolescents with obesity remain so.
  • Hall et al., Cell Metab 2019;30:67 — ultra-processed diet, ~500 kcal/day more eaten ad libitum; n=20, four weeks.
  • Hollands et al. (Cochrane 2015), portion, package and tableware size — experimental review: larger portions/packages/tableware increase consumption.
  • Rolls et al., Am J Clin Nutr 2002;76:1207 — larger portions increased energy intake with no matching rise in fullness.
  • Fothergill et al., Obesity 2016;24:1612 ("Biggest Loser" follow-up) — resting metabolic rate suppressed six years on; 13 of 14 regained.
  • GBD 2017 Diet Collaborators, Lancet 2019;393:1958 — ~11 million deaths/year attributable to dietary risks.
  • Aune et al., Int J Epidemiol 2017 — fruit/vegetable benefit continuing to ~800 g/day.
  • PREDIMED (Estruch et al., NEJM 2018) — ~30% relative reduction in major cardiovascular events; retracted and republished after randomisation irregularities.

Movement

  • WHO 2020 guidelines (Bull et al., Br J Sports Med 2020;54:1451) — 150–300 min/week of moderate activity for adults.
  • Ekelund et al., BMJ 2019;366:l4570 — accelerometer meta-analysis, ~36,000 adults; front-loaded dose-response.
  • Paluch et al., Lancet Public Health 2022;7:e219 — ~47,000 adults; most of the mortality benefit captured below the popular round step targets, then plateaus.
  • Klimentidis et al., Int J Obes 2018;42:1161 — genome-wide association study of physical activity, providing the genetic instruments used in Mendelian-randomisation analyses.
  • Choi et al., JAMA Psychiatry 2019;76:399 — two-sample Mendelian randomisation: physical activity causally lowers the risk of major depression.
  • Stevinson et al., Int J Behav Med 2015;22:170 — qualitative study of parkrun: free, inclusive, social features drive participation.
  • Haake et al., Health Promot Int 2022;37 — parkrun uptake skews to more affluent areas (≈7× a deprived one), so provision alone is not equitable.

Sleep

  • Crowley, Acebo & Carskadon, Sleep Med 2007;8:602 — adolescent circadian phase delay across puberty.
  • Paruthi et al., J Clin Sleep Med 2016;12:785 (AASM consensus) — 8–10 hours per night for 13–18-year-olds.
  • Dunster et al., Sci Adv 2018;4:eaau6200 — Seattle school start delayed 55 minutes; actigraphy-measured sleep +34 minutes.
  • Drake et al., J Clin Sleep Med 2013;9:1195 — caffeine 0/3/6 hours before bed measurably reduced sleep.
  • Chang et al., PNAS 2015;112:1232 — evening light-emitting screens suppressed melatonin and delayed the circadian clock.
  • Qaseem et al., Ann Intern Med 2016;165:125 — ACP guideline: CBT-I is first-line for chronic insomnia.

Sun

  • Green et al., Lancet 1999;354:723 (Nambour) — 2×2 factorial design, 1,621 participants; daily sunscreen reduced squamous cell carcinoma.
  • Green et al., J Clin Oncol 2011;29:257 (Nambour ten-year follow-up) — melanoma 11 vs 22 (HR 0.50, 95% CI 0.24–1.02); invasive melanoma 3 vs 11 (HR 0.27, 95% CI 0.08–0.97).

Collective / structural action

  • Pell et al., NEJM 2008;359:482 — after Scotland's 2006 smoke-free law, acute coronary syndrome admissions fell 17%, two-thirds in non-smokers.
  • Tan & Glantz, Circulation 2012;126:2177 — meta-analysis of 45 studies; smoke-free laws lowered cardiac, stroke and respiratory hospitalisations.
  • Frazer et al., Cochrane 2016;CD005992 — 77 studies; consistent cardiovascular benefit of smoke-free legislation.
  • Wyper et al., Lancet 2023;401:1361 — Scotland's minimum unit pricing associated with a 13.4% fall in wholly-alcohol-attributable deaths.
  • O'Donnell et al., BMJ 2019;366:l5274 — minimum unit pricing cut off-trade alcohol purchased by ~7.6%.
  • Scarborough et al., PLoS Med 2020;17:e1003025 — UK soft drinks levy: drinks above the sugar threshold fell ~34 percentage points (reformulation).
  • Rogers et al., PLoS Med 2023;20:e1004160 — soft drinks levy: ~1.6 percentage points lower obesity in Year-6 girls, largest in the most deprived quintile.
  • Goodman et al., Am J Public Health 2014;104:e38 — new walking/cycling routes: more activity at two years near routes (weaker, non-randomised evidence).

Deliberately not cited

  • Any figure for the share of UK gambling-industry revenue derived from harmed players. The concept is well supported; the numbers in circulation vary widely and several trace to advocacy sources. Take it from Gambling Commission participation data or leave it out.
